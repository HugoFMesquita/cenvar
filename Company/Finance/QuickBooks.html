How To Receive an E-Check in Quickbooks

  1. Go into Job Progress to the “Finance” section of the account you are trying to receive the check on.
  2. Go to the “Payments Received” field and press the “+” button.
  3. Enter the check information and save the payment.
  4. Go into QuickBooks to hit drop-down for customers and click into the “Customer Center”.
  5. Search for the customer by name. (Note: Payments may take a few minutes to come over from Job Progress)
  6. Double click the payment that you put into Job Progress.
  7. Click “E-Check” once the payment screen opens.
  8. A new window will open and will ask for the routing number & the account number, both of which are available at the bottom of the check. Once you have everything filled in, press “Process Payment”.
  9. Keep the checks received as E-Checks in an office file so that if we need to revisit one, we still have them.

 

QuickBooks Frequently Asked Questions:

Here you will find helpful tips and frequently asked questions pertaining to the Cenvar Roofing QuickBooks policies and procedures. If you have any questions that you do not see addressed here, please contact Justin Heavner: justin@cenvarroofing.com

  • I have a QuickBooks question. Who should I call? First, check the Wiki for your answer. If the answer is not listed, please contact Justin Heavner: justin@cenvarroofing.com
  • I cannot log into QuickBooks. What do I do? Please contact Justin Heavner: justin@cenvarroofing.com
  • I forgot to bill/charge something on the customers invoice, but I just discovered it a month later. Can I revise the invoice? No. You must never change a customer invoice from a previous month.
  • I need to take something off of a customers invoice. What do I do? You must never adjust a customer’s invoice to a lower amount. You should always issue a Credit Memo with a detailed description explaining the reason why you are issuing a credit.
  • I need to add an item to a customers invoice after we have already sent the invoice. What do I do? You should always create a separate invoice with a detailed description explaining the reason for the additional invoice cost & for any additional billing that is higher than the current invoice.
