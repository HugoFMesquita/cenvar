Scope

Employees

The Education Program applies to full time employees who have completed at least 90 days of continuous employment.

  • Note: Eligibility alone does not provide acceptance in the program.

College & Universities

Liberty University Online (LUO) is the preferred institution. LUO offers a discount to company employees and payment is made directly from the company. Other colleges and universities are accepted, but the employee will have to pay upfront and receive reimbursement after course(s) are completed with a satisfactory grade.

Course(s)/Program of study

Course(s)/Program of study must be applicable to some area of the company. 

Forms

Education Program Application Form

Contractual Commitment to the Company

Limit to Benefit

Employee must maintain a cumulative grade point average (GPA) of a 3.0 or higher ("B") on a 4.0 scale.

Courses that are Pass/Fail must be completed as “Pass.”

Maximum benefit of $7,000 per employee per year is provided.

Benefit includes the cost of books, course-specific supplies, and mandatory fees directly related to the course(s).

Excluded from this policy include general supplies (e.g., pens, pencils, computers), travel expenses, and avoidable charges (e.g., late fees, finance charges).

Application to the program must be completed and approved prior to the start of the course(s).

All course work, study, and homework must be completed during the employee's personal time, not during work hours.

Employee must maintain employment with the company until the end of the course(s).

  • Note: Completion of course(s), program, or degree is not a guarantee for a different role, promotion, or merit increase.

Application Process

Employees must submit a formal request for educational assistance by completing an application form prior to the start of classes, including details of the course, expected expenses, and supporting documentation (see Education Program Application form).

Applications are submitted to the manager of the employee; upon their approval, the manager will submit to Human Resources (HR). Once HR approves, registration can be completed.

Approval is based on eligibility criteria and budget availability. 

Payment

Payment should be made directly from the company to the educational institution for course tuition and fees when LUO was chosen. Tuition and fees for other institutions along with any allowable educational cost must be submitted after the course is complete. Reimbursement will be made within 30 days after receipt submission.

Employees may apply for other forms of financial aid. Payment/reimbursement not to exceed the actual costs of completing course(s)/program(s).

Failure of a course or a cumulative GPA lower than a “B” (3.0) will require repayment to the company by the employee. No additional course(s) will be permitted in the program until repayment has been made.

Employee Commitment

Upon successful completion of a course, employee must sign a one-year contract with the company. If any employee voluntarily leaves the company before fulfilling their one-year commitment, a prorated repayment may be required.

Non-Compliance

Failure to meet the GPA requirements.

Failure to obtain pre-approval prior to the start of class absolves the company of any obligation for the course(s)/program.

Failure to fulfill the contractual obligation to the company may result in the employee being responsible for reimbursing all or part of the funds covered.

Policy Review

This policy is subject to periodic review and may be revised at the discretion of the company.
