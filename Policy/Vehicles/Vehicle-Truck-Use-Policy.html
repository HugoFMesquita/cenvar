Prior to Operation

Every employee who drives a company vehicle must sign a Company Vehicle Use Agreement

A new employee is not permitted to drive any company owned vehicle or allowed to be on the job site until the new employee has been properly on boarded at Corporate.

Employee authorizes the company to obtain and review the Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) of the employee.

Employees who drive a Cenvar Roofing vehicle must have a valid and current driver’s license, are expected to drive in a safe and responsible manner, and to maintain a good driving record. Our Auto Insurance company regularly monitors and reviews DMV records to determine if an employee’s personal driving record indicates a pattern of unsafe or irresponsible driving. If an employee has 3 or more driving infractions within 3 years, our insurance company may deem them uninsurable, and could result in possible termination. Examples of driving infractions include, but are not limited to:

  • moving violations (speeding, driving thru red light, etc)
  • chargeable accidents ( the driver is determined to be the primary cause of the accident through speeding, inattention, etc.)
  • any combination of accidents and/or moving violations

All employees who drive a company vehicle will be required to complete and pass a 2-hour online Defensive Driving course, provided by Cenvar, within 90 days of hire. Failure to do so will deem an employee ineligible to drive a company vehicle, which may result in termination.  https://www.nsc.org/safety-training/online-training

Any employee who is under 25 years of age, and was employed prior to the adoption of this policy, will be required to complete and pass a 2 hour online Defensive Driving course, provided by Cenvar.  https://www.nsc.org/safety-training/online-training

While in Possession of a Company Vehicle

Employees should be familiar with the Truck, Vehicle, Automobile, & Asset Policies, Procedures, & Information.

Employee must maintain a proper and current driver’s license for the type of company vehicle that they are operating and notify their General Manager immediately if they no longer have a valid license. Failure to do so, while continuing to operate a company vehicle, or driving a personal vehicle for company business, will result in termination.

  • No Smoking is permitted in any Cenvar Roofing vehicle.
  • Drivers must always put the automatic transmission trucks in “tow” mode when towing a trailer.
  • Employee will notify their General Manager of any citations received while operating a company vehicle, or a personal vehicle for company business, by the end of the business day.
  • Employees are responsible for any costs incurred due to citations received while driving a company vehicle, or driving a personal vehicle for company business.
  • An employee who drives as part of his/her job, must notify their General Manager within 24 hours if s/he receives any off duty citations that might affect his/her ability to retain a valid driver license.
  • Employee is responsible for maintaining an MVR within established company guidelines. Failure to do so may deem the employee unable to drive a company vehicle, subjecting them to termination.
  • Employee must follow generally accepted safe driving practices and obey traffic regulations.
  • Employee will ensure that all occupants of a company owned vehicle are properly wearing safety belts while the vehicle is in motion.
  • The personal use of company vehicles is prohibited unless expressly authorized in advance, in writing, by an officer of the company.
  • Employee will not make any modification or add equipment (CD players, stereos, cellular phones, etc.) to any company owned vehicles.
  • Employee is responsible for parking vehicles in safe and legal areas off public ways.
  • Vehicles are not to be loaned to any employee that is not allowed to operate company vehicles.
  • Non-employees are not allowed to operate company vehicles.
  • Employee family members are not allowed to be passengers or allowed to operate company vehicles.
  • No hitchhikers are allowed in vehicles.
  • Towing of mobile homes, travel trailers, or any type of recreational or utility trailer is prohibited.
  • The use of alcohol and controlled substances prior to and during operation of any vehicle is prohibited. Employees found to be driving under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol shall be terminated regardless of whether they have received a citation from law enforcement. Employees are subject to drug/alcohol testing at the discretion of their General Manager based on reasonable suspicion.
  • Any hazardous substances, chemicals or dangerous goods (as defined by law) are prohibited from being carried in a company vehicle.
  • As an employee, I am aware that the company’s automobile insurance DOES NOT cover me when I am driving a company vehicle for personal use that is not authorized in writing, and I indemnify and hold harmless the company should a claim be made against the company for any liability arising out of my unauthorized personal use of a company vehicle.
  • As an employee, I am aware that the company’s automobile insurance DOES NOT cover me when I am driving a non-company vehicle for personal use, it only insures the company vehicles.

Accidents and Citations

Employees will be disqualified from driving vehicles for company purposes if there is any combination of two or more moving violations and/or at fault accidents in a 12 month period. The 12 month time period will run from the calendar date of the first offense. The following are exceptions and will disqualify the employee from driving a company vehicle on the first offense:

  • Leaving the scene of an accident without stopping to file a report
  • Concealing an accident or citation
  • Driving while intoxicated or under the influence of drugs
  • Refusing to take a breathalyzer test
  • Passing a stopped school bus
  • Careless or reckless driving that results in injury to persons or property
  • Employees within their first 12 months of employment (first 365 days) with Cenvar could be terminated
  • At the discretion of the General Manager based on the nature or seriousness of the offense

All accidents involving Company vehicles or assets, regardless of severity, must be reported by the involved employee to their General Manager immediately. If an employee is involved in an accident while in a company vehicle, follow the procedures in the Vehicle Accident Policy. Failure to report vehicle accidents or damages, may result in disciplinary action.

Cenvar reserves the right to require an employee to pay damage costs incurred due to an at fault accident up to $10,000, payable over a three year period through payroll deduction. If employment ends prior to the debt being satisfied, the employee will still be responsible for the costs or be subject to legal action.

Any Cenvar employee who is in their first year of employment (first 365 days) and who has an at fault accident while driving a company vehicle, or while driving a personal vehicle for company use, will be terminated.

Any employee who has had an at fault accident or received a citation while driving a company vehicle or driving a personal vehicle for company use, in the last 2 years will be required to complete and pass a 4 hour online Defensive Driving course, provided by Cenvar. https://www.nsc.org/safety-training/online-training

Any employee who has an accident in a company vehicle, or while operating a personal vehicle for company use, may be required to take a defensive driving course, at the discretion of Cenvar. This is regardless of “at fault status.”  https://www.nsc.org/safety-training/online-training

Any employee who is required to complete a Defensive Driving course, must provide proof of completion and passing score to their General Manager within 90 days of assignment.  https://www.nsc.org/safety-training/online-training

Driver Incentive Program: 

In an effort to: 

  • Reduce accidents & create safer roadways
  • Lower insurance premiums
  • Reduce operating costs
  • Increase productivity for both drivers & vehicles
  • Improve motivation, morale & retention of drivers
  • Reward deserving employees

Cenvar will be implementing a driver incentive program. Each employee, excluding Sales Representatives, who drives a company vehicle or a personal vehicle for company use, for duties as defined in their job description and who remains accident and citation free for one year will be awarded one extra paid vacation day. After two years of no accidents or citations, the employee will be awarded two extra paid vacation days. After three years of no accidents or citations, the employee will be awarded three extra paid vacation days. For each subsequent year of remaining accident and citation free, the employee will continue to be awarded three extra paid vacation days per year.

This incentive will be based on a calendar year running January 1st through December 31st with the awarded day(s) effective January 1st of the following year. Awarded days are to be used according to the current vacation policy. This policy will be retroactive with calculations starting January 1, 2022; awarded days will be effective January 1, 2023. This program is not prorated; new employees will begin eligibility on January 1st of each year. 

Sales Representatives will be subject to the same terms and conditions, however they will be awarded monetarily. Awards will be as follows: 

  • One year, $250 Visa gift card
  • Two years, $500 Visa gift card
  • Three years, $750 Visa gift card

Any employee who does not complete and pass an assigned Defensive Driving course in the 90 day allotted time frame will not be eligible, regardless of driving history. 

General Managers Are Also Subject to the Following Additional Policies: 

The General Manager will be responsible for notifying Human Resources on the Human Resources cat channel:

  • By the end of the business day if an employee notifies them that they no longer have a valid driver’s license.
  • By the end of the business day if an employee notifies them that they have received a citation while operating a company vehicle or a personal vehicle for company business.
  • By the end of the business day if an employee notified them that they have received a citation while off duty that might affect their ability to retain a valid driver’s license.
  • Within one hour if they have used their discretion to send an employee for drug/alcohol testing based on reasonable suspicion.

The General Manager must follow the Vehicle Accident Policy with regards to making notification of employee accidents.

When employees complete the online defensive driving course, the General Manager must submit the proof of completion and passing score to the Human Resources chat channel within one business day of receipt.

On the first Monday of every month, General Managers will incorporate a topic and formal training that pertains to safe driving into their safety meeting and have employees sign a Safety Meeting Attendance Sheet to acknowledge the training. The sheet will be turned into the Human Resources channel on Google Chat by close of business the next day. This will create a trail of accountability and heighten awareness.

Once per quarter, General Managers will review the glove box contents of company vehicles with employees during the Monday safety meeting. General Managers and employees will sign a Safety Meeting Attendance Sheet acknowledging this has been checked and turn the sheet in on the Human Resources channel on Google Chat. This will ensure they know what to do in the event of an accident, create a trail of accountability and heighten awareness.
