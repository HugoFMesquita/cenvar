Safety Enforcement and Fines / Termination of Employment

General Managers, Managers, and Regional Managers shall do safety inspections every time they visit a jobsite. If they find a violation, the offending employee shall be written up, using the Safety Violation Citation Form, and fined.

Please contact the Cenvar Roofing Safety Compliance Officer with questions regarding company safety policies.

Primary Safety Policies

Every employee on any active reroof is in a harness that is properly fastened to a lanyard.

All lanyards have adequate slack taken out so they would arrest a fall.

The anchor points are properly nailed into the framing.

There are a maximum of two mechanics hooked per anchor point.

Fines for primary policies

  • 1st offense: 1 week mandatory suspension from work without pay. Vacation pay is not allowed to be used during any mandatory suspension.
  • 2nd offense: Mandatory termination (resets annually)
  • If a Cenvar Roofing employee is onsite with a subcontractor and the crew is out of compliance, the disciplinary action to the employee will be the same as if he was out of compliance himself.

Secondary Safety Policies 

Ladders are extended 3 feet above roof line.

Ladders have a properly installed standoff.

Ladders must have standoffs.

Employees must wear safety glasses when performing roofing work.

Repair Department: When shingles are being removed, employees must anchor up.

Fines for secondary policies

  • 1st offense: Write up.
  • 2nd offense: 1 day pay or termination (resets annually)

Safety Violations

The Safety Director is required to post all Subcontractor / Crew violations in the General Manager channel, and also to the Human Resources channel.

The Safety Director is required to post all employee safety violations in the General Manager channel, and also to the Human Resources channel.

Safety Violation Citation

The Cenvar Roofing Safety Requirements document is signed and acknowledged by every employee during the employee onboarding process. This document is a detailed list of safety requirements that must be abided by.

Employee Cenvar Roofing Safety Requirements Doc

Section A:

OSHA approved safety harnesses must be worn by every employee on an active reroof job. They must be attached to an OSHA approved rope to a properly installed OSHA approved anchor point. Rock climbing harnesses may be used on a 9/12 pitch or more. When using a rock climbing harness, the rope MUST stay taught.

  1. Rock climbing harnesses are not considered a fall arrest system, but a fall restraint system. Note: per OSHA and ANSI Z359, never use a body belt in a fall arrest application. Body belts are acceptable for fall restraint only.
  2. Harnesses must be properly cinched and adjusted.
  3. Safety ropes must be properly adjusted at all times so that they will arrest a fall.
  4. No more than two employees may attach to one 10k lb. anchor point at any time. Make sure the anchors that are being used are rated for at least 5,000 lbs. per anchor per person. Manufacturer instructions must be followed on securing the anchor to the roof.
  5. Only one man is allowed to go up to mount the first anchor. He must mount the first anchor immediately upon getting on the roof, tie himself off immediately to set the rest of the anchors, and then attach additional ropes to allow the other employees to get on the roof.
  6. Anchors must be installed every 15-20' along the ridge line.
  7. When getting onto the roof, every employee must attach and adjust his rope before getting off the ladder onto the roof.
  8. Every employee must get completely onto the ladder with both feet before detaching from his rope when getting off the roof.
  9. When finishing a job, only one employee may remain on the roof to take out the last anchor and finish the last section.
  10. Hard hats must be worn while working on the ground within 30' of a building if there is any construction, construction preparation, or if any person or material is on the roof.
  11. Ladders shall be set every 20 feet at the eaves to make dismounting the roof safer.
  12. All ladders must be tied off to an anchor point for the duration of the job.
  13. The first man up, and the last man off the roof, must have a helper hold the bottom of the ladder because it's not tied off yet.
  14. Ladders, when set up on hard surfaces, must have two bundles of shingles on feet to prevent kick out.
  15. When carrying tools or material up the ladder, they must be handed from the ladder to the roof, to an employee already in a harness. Recommend: Must have three-point contact at all times when ascending and descending a ladder.
  16. Safety glasses must be worn at all times while operating a nail gun or power saw.
  17. All safety violations carry escalating fines, and finally termination! See the Team Guide for specifics.

Section B

Preceding requirements do not apply for inspections or pre construction sales appointments. Section B applies to all Cenvar Roofing employees.

  1. Ladders shall have sufficient angle that a man with his toes against the feet of the ladder can just touch the ladder with his arms extended straight forward.
  2. Ladders must be extended above the roof edge a minimum of 36". If this is not possible a grab rail must be installed at the roof edge.
  3. Ladder stabilizers (Stand Offs) must be installed at the top of every ladder.
  4. Never do anything that makes you feel unsafe, even if it is technically allowed by our safety policies.

Section C

Section C applies only to Inspectors and Sales Personnel.

  1. Never walk on cedar shakes, slate, or asbestos roofing.
  2. Always wear cougar paw shoes for roof pitches from 7/12 to 8/12.
  3. When getting on roof pitches from 9/12 to 12/12, only walk on valleys, hips, and ridges.
  4. Never walk on pitches steeper than 12/12.

Section D

Section D applies to all Cenvar Roofing employees.

  1. Aggressive driving including speeding, tailgating, lane weaving, and running red lights or stop signs is not tolerated.
  2. Use of a hand-held cell phone while operating a vehicle - whether the vehicle is in motion or stopped at a traffic light is prohibited. This includes answering or making phone calls, engaging in phone conversations, and reading or responding to emails, instant messages, tweets, and text messages.
  3. Drivers are required to honor posted speed limits.
  4. All vehicle cargo (truck, van, trailer, equipter, etc.) must be secured at all times while driving.
