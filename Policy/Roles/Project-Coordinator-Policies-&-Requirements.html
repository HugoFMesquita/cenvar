Project Coordinator Uniform

To maintain a professional image, uniforms will be worn from the waist up.

No badly torn pants or shorts are allowed.

Neatly trimmed facial hair is allowed.

Employees must not appear disheveled and must shave daily.

All hats that are worn by any Cenvar employee while on the job must be either Cenvar issued or 100% unbranded, logo-ed, or messaged in any way.

Project Coordinator Training

  • Project Coordinator Training Program: The Cenvar Roofing Project Coordinator Training Program is professional development program for all PC’s to gain knowledge and hands on experience.
  • Project Coordinators are required to pass a Cenvar Roofing closed book Owens Corning Technical Aptitude Test.
    • The OC Technical Aptitude Test is a 40 question multiple choice test.
    • Each question is worth 2 points, for a total of 80 possible points.
    • Your General Manager will arrange a date / time for your testing in your branch.
  • All PCs are required to complete all Owens Corning: OC University training modules within the first 4 weeks of their hire date AND again within month 6-8 after hire date.
  • Forklift Certification / Forklift Training: All Cenvar Roofing Project Coordinator, and members of the Repair Crew, and Shop Crew are required to be Forklift Certified.

Project Coordinator Customer Policies

PCs are required to communicate with customer using only company provided mediums (email, cell phone, etc.). Communication with the customer via personal cell phone / email is not allowed.

Project Coordinator Subcontractor / Crew Policies

PCs shall only be responsible for the production of the current crews they manage, and for doing go backs for any job they managed. They are not to be assigned or paid for any other duties.

PCs are not allowed to take their company truck or any company vehicle home.

It is the PC's responsibility to take their crew to fix a go-back leak or issue. 

  • Take the appropriate amount of crew members to complete the job.
  • Notify your GM of the issue.

PCs are required to get on the roof when possible and will be held accountable for quality of installation. 

  • The PC must follow all Cenvar Roofing safety policies and procedures, and must be in fall protection at all times when on the roof.
  • Note: The subcontractor crew's foreman is solely responsible for the health and safety conditions on the job. Cenvar has no responsibility for monitoring those conditions.

PCs must provide the subcontractor crew all job payment information on each job the crew completes based on the information that they input. 

  • It is not required to have the subcontractor signature on the pay sheet.
  • PCs are required to add a $300 Trip Charge on the crew pay sheet when a special trip is used.

Subcontractors will only get paid for wood replacement that they take before and after photos of. PCs are responsible for uploading the before and after wood replacement photos to Job Progress, and will be included in the job audit. *See also Subcontractor / Crew Policy

All subcontractor/crew issues with pay must be resolved by the Project Coordinator first before it is escalated to the General Manager.

PCs are required to fill out the current week's Subcontractor payroll sheet once a job is completed in order for their Subcontractor to be paid accurately. Specifically, the PC must daily fill out payroll by 12:00 pm (noon) the following day.

Before, During, and After The Job

Before the Job

Check over the next job in your schedule the day before you start it. Check the salesman's math on the roof drawing, check the material list to make sure it matches the drawing, call the salesman if you notice anything unusual, make a list of any special tools or materials you will need for the morning.

When you arrive on the job site, measure the roof and check it against the salesman's drawing.

Send a message via your branch Google Chat channel to the GM and the salesman to inform about any mistakes you find in the salesman's work or measurements.

Project Coordinators are no longer required to be at the job site first thing in the morning before the crew, but are encouraged to do so when possible. The Project Coordinator is required to call the customer the day before to set expectations. Upon arrival to the customer's property, (if the customer is home) do a walk around with the customer to acknowledge pre-existing damage, confirm details of the roofing contract (example: not doing porch on back), and discuss any abnormal things with the customer.

Introduce the homeowner to at least crew foreman, preferably the whole crew.

Ask customers if they would like to receive photos of the progress of the crew or any damaged decking.

Address any concerns that the customer may have.

During the Job

All reused vents noted in the customer contract must be closely inspected for defects.

Before the job is finished, notify the office manager so that an invoice can be created and emailed to the customer. Notify the Office Manager if the customer needs a paper copy of the invoice.

Project Coordinators are required to leave two unopened tarps behind with the crew when they leave and the roof is still open. This policy includes when the PC leaves the job temporarily to get material. The PC will need to relocate those tarps the next day to make sure they are still intact. Tarps that are used for purposes other than rain will be charged back to the crew.

Project Coordinators must monitor the weather, and keep their truck caulk supply in heated (or at least not freezing) storage when the temperatures drop below 32 degrees Fahrenheit (0 degrees Celsius).

After the Job

PCs are required to make a follow up telephone call the day after completing each job:

  • Ask if the customer has "any questions or concerns".
    • If the customer has questions, the PC will answer their questions over the telephone.
    • If the customer has concerns, the PC will offer to visit the job site to address them.
  • Make the customer aware of any damaged wood found or any changes with the roof.

Effective 11/21/22, Project Coordinators are required to send Bird Eye review requests for 100% of jobs.

If your manager requires it, you must fill out the Subcontractor / Crew Pay Summary and have the crew sign the form to verify the information.

It is your responsibility that all leftover material is returned to the shop, and that all unopened material is returned to distribution.
