Services include

We do all types of residential roofing.

We do commercial shingle roofing.

We do replace rotten decking.

We do build crickets behind all chimneys over 24” for shingles and 30” for Metal.

We do set up scaffolding to protect landscaping for additional $$.

We do take extra precautions to protect K-guard or copper gutters.

We do install the window part of the sun tunnel. 

We do refer electricians to wire fans.

We do siloxane masonry (siloxane will not seal cracks).

We do Geo-seal tops of chimneys if reachable by a ladder, and if the customer signs a disclaimer saying we are not chimney experts and absolving us of liability if the chimney cap leaks.

Excluded from our services

We do not install diverters on shingle roofs.

We do not install the tunnel or interior trim, nothing below the roof deck

We do not caulk cracks in masonry.

We do not replace siding. We do replace fascia and soffit.

We do not wrap soffit or fascia. We only do full gutter replacement and rotten fascia replacement. If rotten fascia is wrapped we will re-wrap it, otherwise no wrapping.

We do not install soffit vents in vinyl soffit.

We do not do chimney caps.

We do not do framing of any kind.

We do not do asbestos work.

We do not install skylights where there were none before, we only replace skylights. 

We do not sell anything “out of the ordinary”. We write on the work orders as little as possible. The work orders should only contain numbers, check marks, and warnings about something a customer is finicky about (landscaping, etc.).

We do not do gutter repairs unless we have also done the full gutter installation.

Single and Double Wide Trailers

We only service single and double wide trailers that currently have shingles. 

Single & Double Wide Trailers

  • We will replace shingle for shingle.
  • We will replace shingle for metal.
  • We will not replace metal for shingle.
  • We will not replace metal for metal.

Forfeiture of Warranty Cases

If we ever install a roof at the customer’s request that is not to the manufacturer’s specs, we MUST write a disclaimer that acknowledges that they have been informed that it is wrong and that neither we nor the manufacturer can stand behind it.

We must inform the customer that the specific service is against manufacturer policy.
