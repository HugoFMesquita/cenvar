Subcontractor Documentation

Every Subcontractor / Crew must maintain all required documentation (workman’s comp, insurance)

No subcontractor / crew shall work for Cenvar roofing without submitting all required documentation:  Subcontractor Agreement , Certificate of Insurance , W-9

New Subcontractors

All new subcontractors / crews will spend their first day with the Cenvar Roofing Quality Control Manager.

All new subcontractors / crews will have their 2nd job inspected shortly after its completion by a member of Quality Control (Juan or Wilmer).

All new subcontractors / crews will will have 2 meetings with the Cenvar Roofing Quality Control Manager on their first day:

  • The first meeting will be with the main crew foreman to discuss policies, collect paperwork, & deliver OC Field Guide.
  • The second meeting will be with the whole crew to go over all items on the sub contract & addendum.

Contractor Safety Handbook

All new subcontractors/crews will be provided with at least one printed (hard copy) of the Contractor Safety Handbook. The Cenvar Roofing Quality Control Manager (or his designee) will give the crew foreman or owner time to review the handbook and ask questions. After review, the foreman or owner must print, sign, and date the Contractor Safety Handbook Acknowledgement form. The completed acknowledgment form needs to be forwarded to corporate with the crew on-boarding documents OR sent directly to the Safety Director. A QR code is provided on the acknowledgment form for the fully electronic (PDF) version of the handbook. The handbook is written in both English (first half of the document) and Spanish (second half of the document).

Work requirements

Any assets rented from Cenvar Roofing must be maintained.

The Subcontractor / Crew shall be held financially accountable for damage to rented equipment that happens due to negligent actions by the sub. General shall be authorized to garnish money owed by the General to the sub to recoup these costs.

The Subcontractor / Crew shall be responsible to transport the debris to the local landfill, the General shall maintain an account there and the sub shall record the job number on the ticket.

The Subcontractor / Crew shall be responsible for all jobsite cleanup.

The Subcontractor / Crew must send a photo of the plywood that they replace (both before and after) to the Project Manager. Sub will only get paid for wood replacement that they take before and after photos of. *See also Project Coordinator Policies

The Subcontractor / Crew shall be responsible to fix, entirely at its expense, all leaks, damage caused by leaks,any installation that is not to the manufacturer’s and Cenvar Roofing Standards specifications, and any installation that is sloppy or is not neat and straight.

The Subcontractor / Crew shall be responsible to follow all state and OSHA safety regulations. The Sub shall be responsible for any and all fines incurred from OSHA as a result of their workers not being in compliance with OSHA safety standards.

The Subcontractor / Crew will be paid for actual square footage.

The Subcontractor / Crew will be fined for not doing any item that is listed on the sub contract including the addendum. The individual fines for subcontractors not complying with an item on the contract/addendum will be left up to the determination of the General Manager.

Roofing Minimum Age Requirement

Roofing workers, whether employees or subcontractors, must be over 18 years of age. Youth less than 18 years old are prohibited from doing roof work, including ground helpers. This is Federal and state law. There is typically an exemption if the youth is in a valid work apprenticeship program for roofing. 

Code of Virginia:

  • § 40.1-100. Certain employment prohibited or limited.
  • A. No child under 18 years of age shall be employed, permitted, or suffered to work:
  • 6. In any capacity in or about excavation, demolition, roofing, wrecking, or shipbreaking operations;

Fact Sheet #74: 

The Employment of Youth in Roofing Occupations and On Roofs under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) - more information can be found in the full document linked below but here are the highlights:

  • Hazardous Occupations Order No. 16 (HO 16), generally prohibits minors less than 18 years from employment in any roofing occupation-on a roof as well as on the ground-as well as any work requiring the youth to work on or about a roof.
  • The term roofing occupations means all work performed in connection with the installation of roofs, including related metal work such as flashing, and applying weatherproofing materials and substances (such as waterproof membranes, tar, slag or pitch, asphalt prepared paper, tile, composite roofing materials, slate, metal, translucent materials, and shingles of asbestos, asphalt, wood or other materials) to roofs of buildings or other structures. The term also includes all jobs on the ground related to roofing operations such as roofing laborer, roofing helper, materials handler and tending a tar heater
  • The rules allow 16- and 17-year-olds who are employed pursuant to a valid apprenticeship or student-learner program to perform work that would otherwise be prohibited.
  • When state child labor laws differ from the federal provisions, an employer must comply with the higher standard.
