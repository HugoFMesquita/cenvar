Once a customer sales presentation has been completed and the quote has been sent to the customer, the job is moved to the Estimated: Sales F/U Required stage in Job Progress.

Proposal on Hold: 

This stage is to be used if the Sales Representative is waiting for an Eagle View, material pricing, etc.

Once the estimate is sent to the customer, the job must be moved to the Estimated: Sales F/U Required stage.

When a job is added to this stage, both a follow up note and follow up calendar event must be created. The exact follow up procedure must be followed every time, for every job.

Estimated: Sales F/U Required: The sales representative has 7 calendar days to follow up with the customer.  

The job remains in this stage for a maximum of 7 calendar days from the date of quote.

  • This is the stage for fresh / new leads.

It is the responsibility of the Sales Representative to make multiple follow up attempts within the 7 calendar days, and document each telephone call / attempt with a separate Follow-up Call Note.  

When a job is added to this stage, both a follow up note and follow up calendar event must be created. The exact follow up procedure must be followed every time, for every job.

If the customer signs the contract, the job is moved to the Signed Contract stage.

If the customer has not decided to sign the contract but there is a possibility that they will sign the contract, the job is moved to the Estimated: Additional Follow Up Required stage for future follow up calls. 

If the customer is not ready to sign the contract yet, but is indicating they would like to be contacted at a future date more than 30 days out, the job is to be moved to the Possible Future Work (Quoted) stage. 

Estimated: Additional Follow Up Required:

This stage is the responsibility of the sales representative to follow up regularly with the customer.  

  • Sales promotions will be sent to all customers in this stage.
  • Any job in this stage is required to have both a ‘Follow Up Call Note” (not Job Note) and a "Follow Up Reminder calendar event." 
  • When a job is added to this stage, both a follow up note and follow up calendar event must be created. The exact follow up procedure must be followed every time, for every job.

If a Sales Representative is unable to successfully connect with a customer, they are required to make customer sales follow-up phone calls twice a week for the first 4 weeks, followed by once a week for an additional 2 weeks, before archiving the account.

The sales representative must create a follow-up call note in Job Progress detailing each telephone and email attempt.

After 6 weeks, if the customer still has not signed, the sales representative must archive the quote in Job Progress with a Job Note detailing why it was moved or archived, or move it into Possible Future Work (Quoted) if the customer has requested contact at a future date.  

It is the responsibility of the Sales Representative to continue to follow up with the customer regularly, move it into the Possible Future Work (Quoted) stage if the customer asks to be contacted at a date further out than 30 days, or to archive it.

Branch General Managers will also closely monitor this stage.

 Possible Future Work (Quoted): 

There is no maximum number of days that a job can remain in this stage as long as there are current notes and a customer follow up calendar event.

  • Example: The customer has asked the sales representative to call back at a date 8 months later.
  • All quotes where the customer has indicated that they would like specific action from the salesperson at a date more remote than 30 days are put into this stage.
  • Every job in this stage must have a detailed ‘Follow-up Call Note” (not Job Note) explaining the future action the customer requested.
  • Upon moving the quote into this stage, the Salesperson must create a "Follow Up Reminder calendar event" in Job Progress prompting the action the customer requested.
  • Any job in this stage is required to have both a "Follow Up Call Note” (not Job Note) and a "Follow Up Reminder calendar event" within 30 calendar days from the sales appointment. The exact follow up procedure must be followed every time, for every job.
  • Sales promotions will be sent to all customers in this stage.
  • Sales Representatives can enter a maximum Follow Up date of 1 year.

It is the Sales Representatives responsibility to determine if a job is ‘dead’ (a job is considered dead if the job has no note calling for future action by the Sales Representative, and the corresponding reminder for action).  Once the job is ‘dead’, the Sales Representative must Archive the job in Job Progress, and add a Job Note detailing why it was archived. 

Any job that does not fit into the above job stages must be archived in Job Progress with the Job Flag “Job Lost”, and a Job Note detailing why it was archived. The Cenvar Roofing Inventory Management Sheet is critical to ensure that all product & materials are accounted for daily.
