The Cenvar Roofing Insurance Process is used by Sales Representatives who have, upon inspection, found roof damage, and the customer elects to file an Insurance Claim with their Insurance provider.

Sales Representative: attend the scheduled roof inspection appointment.

Sales Representative: perform the roof inspection.

  • Roofing Inspection Form

Sales Representative: document any and all damage.

  • Roofing Inspection Form

Sales Representative: complete the Insurance Proposal / Contract.

Homeowner: will file the insurance claim with their insurance company, list Cenvar Roofing as the roofer, and provide the insurance company the Cenvar Roofing Sales Representative contact information.

  • Sales Representative Name
  • Sales Representative Email
  • Sales Representative Telephone
  • Branch address

Sales Representative: meet with both the homeowner and the homeowner’s insurance adjuster for an insurance inspection.

Homeowners insurance adjuster: approve all roof repairs, and provide an estimate to the homeowner.

Sales Representative: work closely with the insurance adjuster to:

  • negotiate the work to be performed on the proposal / contract.
  • revise the roofing proposal / contract to the dollar amount that is acceptable and agreeable to all parties.

Cenvar Roofing: complete the agreed upon roof repairs / replacement.

  • Sales Representative: send the insurance company a signed Insuranace Certificate of Completion and Release, and proof of any extra work:
    • such as plywood replacement with dollar amount, etc.
  • Sales Representative: negotiate price with the homeowner’s insurance adjuster for extra work performed, to a dollar amount that is acceptable and agreeable to all parties.
  • Cenvar Roofing: sends the final invoice to the homeowner for full payment.
  • Cenvar Roofing: collect payment.

The Insurance Proposal & Contract is used for all jobs that will have an insurance claim filed with the customer’s insurance provider. The Insurance Proposal can also found in Job Progress.

The Certificate of Completion and Release form is used when the agreed upon work has been completed for a job that will be filed with the customer’s insurance provider for an insurance claim.  The Certificate of Completion and Release form can also found in Job Progress.
