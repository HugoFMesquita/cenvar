How to make a new weekly payroll sheet:

You should already have access to the Google folder that you will be adding your pay sheets to.

  • If you do not have access, you will need to request access from the Finance / Human Resources team.
  • Justin Heavner: justin@cenvarroofing.com or 434-610-9657

Make a copy of the Template as this is where all new versions will be updated.

  • NOTE: Making a copy of previous pay sheets can result in payroll errors.

To make a copy, press file, then select the option “make a copy”.

Rename the file to the last date of the current week

Make sure it is in your branches payroll folder

Check the box to share it with the same people.

Press ok to make a new copy.

How to fill out hours per position:

Project Coordinators:

For each PC that you have, please fill in the exact hours that they worked the previous week on the “(Newest) Project Managers” tab.

  • This will allow for their bonuses and pay to populate.

For new PMs, pay will only populate when you let Justin know to update the pay sheet to reflect these changes.

If there are any sick or vacation days, simply leave the day blank and fill in the notes section on the “payroll” tab to notify them of the situation.

When all hours are plugged in, the hours and bonuses will populate on the “Payroll tab” to generate pay.

 

Repair Team:

Use the repair division tab when filling in the payroll for your repair team.

NOTE: There are 2 parts to correctly calculate your repair teams pay.

Fill in the 1st section with all the repair billing that the crew worked on during the last week.

  • There are several categories you can place it in under the total billed to help you account for each specific situation.

The repairman are located directly underneath of the total billed which allows you to allocate a specific % to each repairman for what they billed.

PLEASE NOTE THAT ALL BILLING SHOULD NOT HAVE AN ALLOCATION OF MORE THAN 32% to each branch’s repair teams.

  • Anything that is over will result as an overpayment as part of the piecework section.

Fill in the 2nd section with the total hours for each repairman that they worked in the previous week.

All repairman are hourly and are directly affected when hours are not populated.

When all of the hours plugged in, the pay sheets will calculate a bonus and send it over to the “Payroll Tab” for each repairman.

DO NOT POPULATE the payroll tab manually with hours or bonuses as this could result in payroll mistakes.

Subs Payout Structure

Use the Subcontractor Contract information to populate their specific payout structures.

When entering the name on the “Payroll” tab, the name of the sub should automatically populate in this sheet.

Once you have agreed upon pricing with the sub, simply fill out this sheet so that the correct payout will generate for each specific subcontractor.

The correct payout will automatically populate using the data that you have keyed into the sheet.

 

Populating Subs tabs with Jobs:

  • Every subcontractor should have an individual, corresponding tab for you to fill in their specific job information that they completed last week.
  • To add a new subcontractors:
    • Right click on a spare tab in your sheet
    • Rename the tab to correspond with the subcontractor name.
  • Fill in the form with all necessary information that was used on the job.

 

Jobs Overview Section- (All Sections Required)

Color- Use the dropdown to select the correct color of the roof

Project Manager- Use dropdown to select the pc that worked the job

Salesman- Select the correct salesman for the job

Subcontractor- Select the correct contractor that worked the specific job

Date Job Complete- Select the day completed

Check Collected- Select whether the PM received payment for the job after completion

Actual Days- Fill in how many days the crew took to complete the job

Estimated Sq- Fill in how many squares the sales rep estimated for shingles

Estimated days- Fill in how many days the sales rep estimated

Subcontractor Pay Detail:

Fill in the necessary fields that correlate with how many squares are completed.

Project Coordinators are responsible for filling this out each week.

Go to Job Progress and find what was estimated for walkable, steep, etc.

Make sure that if the roof is metal that it is going under the metal section of the pay sheet as that gets different payout rates.

Any returned material should NOT be included in the payout of labor as well.

Material Detail:

Fill in all material that was ordered for the job.

This can be found on the materials section in Job Progress.

Input all returned or extra materials in the 2nd column.

NOTE: Please do not include all the extra materials that are already on your truck, but just what is leftover from the job.

This way we have an accurate picture of how materials were handled on each job.

 

Once everything is filled in, make sure to check the labor % to see if it is under 31%. Anything that is over needs to be double checked to see what went wrong with labor estimates.
