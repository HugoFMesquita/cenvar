Federal Tax Credits

The Residential Clean Energy Credit is a Federal Tax Credit that can be used for renewable energy for a home such as solar, wind, geothermal, fuel cells or battery storage.

The Residential Clean Energy credit equals of 30% of the costs of a new solar system that is installed from 2022 through 2032. The credit decreases to 26% in 2033 and 22% in 2034.

The credit is nonrefundable and cannot exceed the amount you owe in Federal taxes.

Unused credits can be carried forward to reduce Federal taxes owed in future years. It does not include interest paid including loan origination fees.

The credit does not have an annual or lifetime limit.

The credit can be applied to the Federal taxes in the year Solar is activated.

The credit is for solar only and not for the roof replacement or repair.

Form 5695, Residential Clean Energy Credits should be completed and filed with the tax return to claim the credits.

See the IRS for full details about the tax credit.

 

Virginia Solar Renewable Energy Credits (SRECs)

Solar Renewable Energy Credits (SRECs) represent the “green” value of your electricity. They are like a voucher that proves your electricity is produced by a renewable energy source, like solar panels.

The Virginia Clean Economy Act establishes a renewable energy portfolio standard (RPS) that Dominion and AEP produce 100% of their electricity by 2045 & 2050.

Homeowners with solar panels earn 1 SREC for every 1,000 kWh that their solar system produces.

The SREC cannot be sold directly through to the Utility company. They need to be sold through an aggregator, such as Sun Tribe Trading. See Sun Tribe for more information.
